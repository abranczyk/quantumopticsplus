Designing the next generation of quantum-optical technologies

Light is our best medium for sending information over long distances—it moves at nature's speed limit, and doesn't degrade for hundreds of kilometers. As quantum technologies become more prevalent, and we strive to transmit quantum information, we will require quantum light.

Careful control of interactions between quantum states and quantum systems within waveguide arrays is an important ingredient in the development of quantum technologies.

We work with light-atom interactions in waveguide QED to study fundamental physics and design new quantum technologies.


Exploring analogue physics using quantum optics

Studying one area of physics through the lens of another makes it possible to leverage existing intuition and techniques in new ways, leading to discoveries perhaps otherwise not possible.

We study analogue physics using quantum-optical interactions mediated by dielectric materials. Dielectrics have enormous application in various technologies (such as the quantum light sources that we design), but they are also fundamentally interesting, since light travelling through a dielectric can behave in strange ways: it can take on an effective mass, bend, slow down, stop, reverse direction, or even transform into a completely different kind of light.

Remarkably, this behaviour can also be interpreted in terms of very different physics. For example, the dielectric can play the role of an effective gravitational field.

We study analogues to discover new physics, as well as generate new ideas for using dielectrics in quantum technologies.
